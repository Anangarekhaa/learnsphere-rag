LearnSphere Infrastructure Overview

1. Cloud Hosting Architecture

LearnSphere is hosted on Amazon Web Services (AWS). Primary production systems operate in the United States within the us-east-1 region.

Infrastructure is deployed using infrastructure-as-code templates to ensure consistency and repeatability.

Production environments are isolated from development and staging environments to reduce risk of data leakage.

2. Network Segmentation

The architecture is segmented into application, database, and administrative layers.

Production databases reside within private subnets and are not directly accessible from the public internet.

Access to administrative interfaces is restricted to authorized IP ranges and requires multi-factor authentication.

Firewall rules restrict inbound and outbound traffic to required services only.

3. Backup and Disaster Recovery

Automated backups of production databases are performed daily.

Backups are encrypted and retained for 30 days. Backup storage is logically separated from live production systems.

Backup restoration procedures are tested annually to validate data integrity and operational readiness.

A disaster recovery plan documents recovery objectives and escalation procedures.

4. High Availability

Application services are deployed across multiple availability zones within the AWS region.

Load balancers distribute incoming traffic across multiple instances to improve reliability and performance.

Health checks automatically remove unhealthy instances from rotation.

5. Change Management

Infrastructure changes follow documented change management processes. Proposed changes are reviewed and approved prior to deployment.

All changes are tracked in version control systems for auditability.
